# Blog Post — Quality Criteria

Score a short blog post or essay (≈300–800 words). Anchors: **50 = average, 70 = good,
90+ = exceptional.** Reward craft, not length — tighten ruthlessly. Award the top of a
band when the post clearly does the right thing.

## Dimensions (total: 100)

### Opening (22)
- The first line earns the second — a concrete image, a claim with stakes, or a real
  question; not a throat-clearing wind-up or a dictionary definition (0-14)
- A reader knows within two sentences why this is worth their time (0-8)

### One clear idea (22)
- The post makes ONE point and lands it; tangents are cut, not parked (0-14)
- The title and the payoff are the same idea (0-8)

### Concreteness (20)
- Claims are grounded in a specific example, number, or scene — not abstraction
  stacked on abstraction (0-12)
- Shows before it tells (0-8)

### Structure & flow (16)
- Each paragraph advances the argument; transitions are earned, not "Furthermore," (0-10)
- Reads in one sitting; no dead middle (0-6)

### Landing (12)
- Ends on a real point or turn, not a summary of what was just said (0-12)

### Voice (8)
- Confident, human, specific; no corporate hedging or AI throat-clearing
  ("In today's fast-paced world…") (0-8)
